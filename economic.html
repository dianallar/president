Economic Policy

An economy that works from the middle, out—not the top down.

Economic Policy Platform

1. Windfall Tax

It's time to ensure that money isn't being taken right out of your wallet and hoarded by fat cats. Allar will tax corporations for extraordinary, exploitation- or crisis-driven profits and redistribute revenues to fund direct relief for families, public investments, and price-stabilizing programs, particularly in essential industries. This policy targets sectors that unfairly profited from emergency conditions, ensures money is returned to the public, and finances affordability measures like rental assistance, rebates on essential goods, and investments in domestic manufacturing.

2. Strategic Mineral Reserve Act

Create national stockpiles of critical minerals and raw materials—lithium, cobalt, rare earths—to reduce supply-chain vulnerabilities from government-sanctioned space mining, secure clean-energy supply chains, and support strategic domestic manufacturing. The Reserve would be managed to stabilize prices, coordinate with allies, and incentivize sustainable domestic space mining and recycling. Space mining is a very sensitive and inflationary industry, we must ensure its protection from vulnerabilities.

3. Requiring compliance with sectoral bargaining in federal contracts

Require companies that win federal contracts to comply with industry-level bargaining agreements or to recognize multi-employer collective bargaining frameworks, ensuring federal dollars support higher standards, predictable wages, and industry-wide protections rather than a race to the bottom.

4. Hiring halls for displaced workers in states with high unemployment

Establish union-coordinated hiring halls and placement centers in regions hit hard by layoffs, giving displaced workers rapid access to reemployment, apprenticeship placements, retraining programs, and wraparound support to ease transitions between jobs.

5. Excess profit recapture (Taxing corporations that raise prices faster than wages)

Introduce a tax on firms whose price increases outpace national wage growth, discouraging opportunistic price hikes and recapturing excess income to fund consumer relief, enforcement resources, and worker-support programs.

6. CEO Pay Ratio Cap of 50:1 for federal contractors and subcontractors

Cap the pay ratio between CEOs and median workers at 50:1 for firms receiving federal dollars, aligning executive compensation with worker pay, discouraging excessive executive pay funded by public contracts, and promoting wage growth across firms serving taxpayers.

7. Triple NLRB Funding

Provide the National Labor Relations Board with significantly expanded funding to speed investigations, modernize enforcement technology, and reduce case backlogs—so unfair labor practices are addressed rapidly and workers can organize without prolonged delay.

8. Corporate death penalty for repeat wage-theft violators

Empower regulators to revoke corporate charters or strip firms of business licenses after repeated, egregious wage-theft offenses, removing repeat offenders from the market and converting abandoned assets to worker-run or community-owned enterprises.

9. Expand monopoly threshold

Tighten antitrust thresholds and update merger review standards to cover modern market realities—platform concentration, common ownership, and data-control—so regulators can block mergers that substantially reduce competition before harm occurs.

10. Harsher penalties for companies that exploit people abroad for product manufacturing

Increase sanctions, import bans, and criminal penalties for firms complicit in forced labor, child labor, or severe rights abuses abroad; require supply-chain audits and public disclosure so consumers and enforcement agencies can hold violators accountable.

11. Produce 50+ Active pharmaceutical ingredients domestically by 2058

Launch a long-term industrial policy to reshore essential pharmaceutical production, invest in public and private manufacturing capacity, and guarantee domestic production of at least 50 key active pharmaceutical ingredients to secure medicine supplies and lower drug shortages.

12. Force divestment of idle patents and real estate

Require firms that hoard patents or leave valuable real estate idle to either commercialize, license, sell, or forfeit those assets—freeing innovation bottlenecks, lowering barriers to competition, and unlocking land for housing and productive use.

13. Ban vertical integration in food and healthcare

Diana Allar will forbid dominant firms from owning multiple levels of the supply chain in critical sectors like food and healthcare—separating production, distribution, and retail—to prevent price extraction, conflicts of interest, and market foreclosure.

14. CEOs may be held personally liable for wage theft

Diana Allar will allow civil and, for severe willful abuses, criminal liability for executives who knowingly enable or conceal systemic wage theft—creating stronger deterrents and ensuring accountability reaches decision-makers, not just corporate shells.

15. Mandate return of bonuses when firms raise prices >2x inflation rate

An Allar Administration will require executive bonus clawbacks when companies knowingly raise prices at rates substantially above general inflation without justified cost drivers; recovered funds would be redistributed to affected consumers and workers.

16. Increase the Land Value Tax

An Allar Administration will implement a progressive land value tax to capture economic rents from landowners, incentivize productive use of land, and generate revenue for public goods, addressing wealth inequality and funding essential services.

17. Criminal penalties for algorithmic price-fixing

Make it a crime to deploy algorithms, collusive pricing bots, or coordinated machine-learning systems that automate price-fixing or tacit collusion across competitors, with penalties for firms and responsible managers.

18. Expand AmeriCare Act model to housing (FedHomes) and utilities

Extend the public-option model to essential services by creating FedHomes as a public housing option and regulated public utilities that provide affordable, transparent rates—leveraging scale to lower costs and guarantee basic services for all.

19. Mandate public cost breakdowns for essential goods

Require sellers of essential goods to publish standardized cost breakdowns—materials, labor, distribution, and margin—so policymakers, regulators, and consumers can see where prices come from and spot unjustified markups.

20. 25% bounty on recovered price-fixing fines

Offer whistleblowers and cooperating state/federal attorneys a 25% bounty on fines recovered from price-fixing cases to incentivize tip-offs, speed enforcement, and increase the resources available for antitrust investigations.

21. Limit student debt payments to 5% of disposable income

Legislate a permanent cap that limits monthly student loan payments to 5% of disposable income to make repayment affordable, reduce defaults, and stabilize household balance sheets—paired with income-driven adjustments for hardship.

22. Forgiveness after 10 years of student debt payments (5 years for public service)

Guarantee widespread loan forgiveness after 10 years of compliant payments, and accelerate forgiveness to 5 years for public-service careers—rewarding socially valuable work and lowering long-term burdens.

23. National 15% interest rate cap

Establish a uniform usury cap of 15% APR on consumer credit products nationwide to protect borrowers from predatory rates while preserving access to responsible lending and encouraging lower-cost public alternatives.

24. Ban junk fees on mortgages/rental applications

Prohibit hidden and excessive application fees in housing finance and rental markets, requiring transparent, reasonable charges only; this reduces housing access barriers and prevents exploitative gatekeeping costs.

25. 3% emergency loans during inflationary spikes

Provide emergency, low-cost 3% loans through public banking channels during defined inflation spikes to help households bridge short-term price pressures without resorting to high-interest payday alternatives.

26. Federal protection for workers withholding payments from fraudulent creditors

Protect workers who withhold payments from creditors or collectors engaged in clear fraud or illegal activity—shielding them from retaliation and creating legal pathways to contest abusive claims.

27. Class-action leverage against abusive lenders

Strengthen class-action tools, statutory damages, and collective enforcement mechanisms so communities can efficiently pursue redress against predatory lenders and share recovered relief across harmed borrowers.

28. Targeted Investment in regions hit by plant closures or industry decline

Deploy focused capital, tax credits, and procurement guarantees to communities facing mass closures—finance new industries, infrastructure, and workforce programs that rebuild local economies sustainably.

29. Public Works Surge, to create jobs working on government projects such as infrastructure advancement and health care (See: Domestic Policies)

Kick off a rapid, large-scale public works program prioritizing infrastructure, care economy expansion, and healthcare facility improvements—creating millions of jobs, rebuilding public assets, and accelerating community-level renewal.

30. Sales Tax Holidays automatically triggered when inflation exceeds 4% for 3 months

Implement automatic temporary sales-tax suspensions on basic goods triggered by sustained inflation to provide immediate consumer relief, especially for low-income households, until price stability returns.
