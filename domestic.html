    14. National Use-Of-Force Standard for Police

    We, as a country, have struggled with police brutality plaguing our society for hundreds of years. From the Tulsa Massacre to more individual cases of the senseless murder of black citizens out of an implicit racial prejudice, to the abuse of women in public and in private out of a sense of authority, it's been apparent for a long time that our police force requires reform to carry out its modern purpose: Keeping our country safe at a municipal level.

    Under an Allar Administration, we will establish a binding nationwide standard governing the use of force by law enforcement. Compliance will be tied to federal law enforcement grants, ensuring adoption across all jurisdictions. The standard will also include transparent reporting requirements to track and publicly disclose use-of-force incidents.

    26. Removing Welfare and AmeriCare Work Requirements

    Under an Allar Administration, work requirements for federal welfare programs, including SNAP, TANF, and Medicaid, will be eliminated. Assistance will be based solely on need, not employment status, recognizing that many recipients face barriers such as disability, caregiving responsibilities, or economic downturns. This reform will reduce bureaucratic hurdles, prevent unnecessary loss of benefits, and ensure that welfare programs fulfill their intended role as a safety net.
